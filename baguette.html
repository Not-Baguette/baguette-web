Why Baguettes are absolutely amazing

A baguette is a type of bread that originated in France and is characterized by its long, narrow shape and crispy crust. It is typically made from basic ingredients such as flour, water, yeast, and salt. The dough is mixed, kneaded, and then allowed to rise before being shaped into the iconic elongated form.

The baguette's unique qualities lie in its texture, taste, and versatility. When properly baked, it develops a thin, golden-brown crust that is delightfully crisp and crackles when you bite into it. The interior of a well-made baguette is light, soft, and airy, with an irregular pattern of small holes, known as the crumb. This texture creates a wonderful contrast with the crust, making each bite a delightful experience for the senses.

One of the baguette's most remarkable features is its simple yet exquisite flavor. The combination of the bread's lightly salted dough and the distinct taste that develops during the baking process results in a subtly tangy and slightly sweet profile. This flavor is often enhanced by a slight note of toasted cereal that arises from the bread's crust.

Another aspect that contributes to the baguette's allure is its versatility. It serves as a staple in French cuisine and can be enjoyed in numerous ways. It makes a perfect accompaniment to a wide range of dishes, such as cheeses, cured meats, soups, and salads. It can be sliced and used as the base for sandwiches or toasted to create crispy crostini. Its lightness and delicate flavor make it an ideal vehicle for showcasing various toppings and fillings, allowing for endless culinary creativity.

Formally speaking, the baguette embodies the essence of French baking tradition and craftsmanship. Its creation requires skill and attention to detail, as it relies on the proper balance of ingredients, precise shaping, and precise baking techniques. Mastering the art of baguette making can take years of practice and dedication.

In French culture, the baguette holds a special place. It is an iconic symbol of everyday life, often seen tucked under the arms of Parisians as they make their way home from a local boulangerie. The sound of a baguette's crust being torn apart or the sight of its crumb peeking through a paper bag evokes a sense of comfort, tradition, and culinary excellence.

How to make a baguette

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams (4 cups) bread flour
  • 10 grams (2 teaspoons) salt
  • 10 grams (2 teaspoons) sugar
  • 7 grams (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 350 milliliters (1 ½ cups) warm water

Instructions:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar in warm water. Sprinkle the yeast over the water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour and salt.
  3. Combine the ingredients: Create a well in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the activated yeast mixture. Gradually incorporate the flour into the liquid, mixing with a wooden spoon or your hands until the dough comes together.
  4. Knead the dough: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment to knead the dough.
  5. First rise: Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it doubles in size.
  6. Shape the baguettes: Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it to a lightly floured surface and divide it into three equal portions. Shape each portion into a rectangular shape by flattening the dough and folding it from the top and bottom towards the center. Then roll the dough tightly from one end to the other to form a baguette shape. Repeat with the remaining portions.
  7. Second rise: Place the shaped baguettes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a baguette pan. Cover them with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30 to 45 minutes, or until they have puffed up.
  8. Preheat the oven: While the baguettes are rising, preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). If you have a baking stone or a baking steel, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
  9. Score the baguettes: Just before baking, use a sharp knife or a razor blade to make diagonal slashes along the top of each baguette. This helps the bread expand and gives it the classic baguette appearance.
  10. Bake the baguettes: Transfer the risen baguettes to the preheated oven. If you have a baking stone or a baking steel, carefully slide the baguettes onto it. Bake for about 20 to 25 minutes, or until the baguettes are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.
  11. Cool and enjoy: Remove the baguettes from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack. Allow them to cool for at least 15 minutes before slicing and enjoying.

Reasons to love baguette

1. Crispy crust

Baguettes have a thin, crispy crust that offers a satisfying crunch when you bite into it. The contrast between the crust and the soft interior is delightful.

2. Light and airy texture

The interior of a baguette is light, airy, and filled with small holes, creating a delicate and fluffy crumb. This texture makes each bite feel light and enjoyable.

3. Versatility

Baguettes are incredibly versatile. They can be enjoyed on their own, served with a variety of toppings, or used as a base for sandwiches and bruschetta. Their neutral flavor profile allows them to complement a wide range of ingredients.

4. Simple ingredients

Baguettes are made from basic ingredients like flour, water, yeast, and salt. The simplicity of the ingredients allows the natural flavors to shine through.

5. Nostalgia

Baguettes are often associated with memories of strolling through the streets of Paris or enjoying a picnic in the French countryside. They can evoke a sense of nostalgia and transport you to a different time and place. Like, remember Ratatouille? Yeah, that but with baguettes!

6. Social aspect

Sharing a baguette with friends and loved ones can create a sense of togetherness and conviviality. Breaking off a piece of crust and passing it around is a simple but meaningful gesture of sharing and enjoyment.

7. Culinary creativity

Baguettes can inspire culinary creativity. From using them as a canvas for various flavors and fillings to incorporating them into innovative recipes, baguettes provide a versatile foundation for exploring different tastes and combinations.

8. Timeless appeal

Baguettes have stood the test of time. Their enduring popularity and timeless appeal reflect their universal charm and ability to satisfy our cravings for a delicious and comforting bread.

Proper Etiquette to eat Baguette

1. Break off a piece

Instead of biting directly into the baguette, it is proper etiquette to break off a small piece of the crust with your hands or a bread knife. This allows you to enjoy the baguette in smaller, manageable portions.

2. Butter sparingly

If butter is served with the baguette, it is considered proper etiquette to apply it sparingly to the piece you've broken off. Avoid slathering the entire slice with butter, as it can overpower the flavor of the bread.

3. Don't overload

When making a sandwich or topping the baguette with ingredients, it is important to avoid overloading it. Keep the toppings balanced and restrained to prevent the baguette from becoming too messy or difficult to handle.

4. Use a bread plate

If a bread plate is provided, it is proper etiquette to place your piece of baguette on the plate rather than directly on the tablecloth or table. This helps maintain cleanliness and prevents crumbs from scattering.

5. Break and eat as you go

Instead of attempting to consume the entire length of the baguette all at once, it is more appropriate to break off small pieces as you eat. This allows you to enjoy the baguette gradually and prevents it from becoming stale or dry.

6. Avoid double-dipping

If you're enjoying the baguette with a dip or spread, it is considered proper etiquette to avoid double-dipping. Take a small amount of the dip onto your plate and use your piece of baguette to scoop it up, preventing the spread from being contaminated.

7. Chew with your mouth closed

When eating baguette, it is important to chew with your mouth closed. This shows respect to those around you and prevents excessive noise or the display of half-chewed food.

8. Take small bites

To ensure ease of eating and prevent crumbs from scattering, it is proper etiquette to take small, manageable bites of the baguette. This allows you to enjoy the bread without making a mess.

9. Enjoy with other dishes

Baguettes are often served as part of a meal. It is recommended to enjoy them alongside other dishes, such as soups, salads, or cheese platters, as they complement and enhance the overall dining experience.

10. Respect cultural customs

If you are dining in a different cultural setting, it is important to be aware of and respect the local customs and traditions associated with eating baguettes or bread in general. This ensures that you are mindful of the cultural etiquette and demonstrates your appreciation for the local customs.
